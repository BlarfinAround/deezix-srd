# OSR Compatibility

Deezix is built on the core mechanics of classic old-school fantasy roleplaying games, so most classic adventures and OSR content run with little adaptation.

Four things differ enough to affect converted play: saving throws, Armor Class, skills, and the classes.

## Saving Throws

Classic saves roll high on a d20 and improve steadily every few levels. Deezix saves roll low on a d6 in three bands (levels 1–4, 5–9, 10–15); the die caps the range at 1-in-6 to 5-in-6 (17%–83%).

- **Low level**: roughly comparable — a 1st-level save lands around 17–50%, against classic's 25–45% — though the d6 is swingier.
- **High level**: classic pulls ahead. Classic characters climb toward 85–95% on their best saves and keep gaining in every category, while Deezix tops out at 5-in-6 and improves only at levels 5 and 10. A classic high-level party shrugs off most saves; a Deezix party of equal level does not, so save-or-die threats stay dangerous through the campaign.
- **Modifiers**: a +1 is one full step (~17%) on a d6 against 5% on a d20, so classic items and spells that grant save bonuses are roughly three times as strong.

## Armor Class and Attacks

Deezix uses ascending AC and progressive, class-based attack bonuses in place of descending AC and attack tables. A classic descending AC converts to ascending by subtracting it from 19 (AC 5 → AC 14).

## Skills

Classic dungeon checks — searching, listening, finding secret doors — sit at a fixed 1-in-6 or 2-in-6 for most characters. Deezix starts General Skills at 1-in-6 but adds a class's key-ability bonus to one of them: a high-WIS Cleric searches at up to 4-in-6 (67%) where classic characters cap near 2-in-6 (33%). A Deezix party finds hidden things and hears danger more reliably than its level suggests.

Thief skills convert from percentile chances to X-in-6 Skill Points. Concentrating points raises a few chosen skills to 5-in-6 (83%) early, rather than climbing slowly from low percentages — but they cap at 5-in-6, short of the 95%+ a high-level classic thief eventually reaches.

## Classes

Deezix classes differ from their classic versions in ways that surface in converted play:

- **Smoother attack progression**: Attack Bonus rises gradually rather than jumping every few levels.
- **Fighter**: Cleave lets a fighter who drops a foe strike another, clearing weak ranks faster.
- **Cleric**: no plate armor, but a prepared spell from 1st level — classic clerics cast nothing until 2nd.
- **Magic-User**: Arcane Lore detects, reads, and identifies magic without spending spells.
- **Thief**: distributed Skill Points and dice-based Backstab replace fixed percentile skills and a damage multiplier.

## Net Effect

Together these changes make a Deezix party somewhat more capable than a classic party of the same level — better skills, reliable low-level casting, a smoother combat curve. This may reduce, though not eliminate, reliance on hirelings and retainers, and may quicken the pace of an adventure written for classic characters.
