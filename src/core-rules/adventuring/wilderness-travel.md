# Wilderness Travel

Wilderness travel is measured in **days** and **miles** rather than turns and feet.

## Travel Speeds

On clear terrain with road or trail:

| Mode                   | Miles per Day                     |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| On foot                | 24 miles/day                      |
| Mounted (riding horse) | 48 miles/day                      |
| Mounted (warhorse)     | 32 miles/day (slower in full kit) |
| Cart                   | 24 miles/day (road only)          |
| River (downstream)     | 40 miles/day                      |

Assumes 8 hours of travel.

::: {.callout-important title="Forced March"}
Traveling more than 8 hours in a day imposes **−1 to all rolls** (exhaustion) until the party takes a full rest.
:::

## Terrain Modifiers

| Terrain                        | Speed Modifier |
| ------------------------------ | -------------- |
| Road                           | ×1½            |
| Clear, city, trail, grasslands | ×1             |
| Forest, hills, desert, broken  | ×⅔             |
| Mountains, jungle, swamp       | ×½             |

Round down to nearest mile.

The referee may apply movement penalties or bonuses based on weather conditions.

## Getting Lost

When traveling without a road, guide, or clear landmarks, there is a chance the party loses their way.

::: {.callout-important title="Getting Lost"}
At the end of each day of off-road travel, the referee rolls **1d6** secretly.

- In clear terrain (plains, open forest): Lost on a **1**.
- In difficult terrain (dense forest, hills, swamp): Lost on a **1–2**.
- In extreme terrain (trackless mountains, storm): Lost on a **1–3**.

A local guide negates the lost check entirely.
:::

**When lost**: The party unknowingly travels in an incorrect direction, discovering their error after a full day of travel or when an expected landmark is missing.

## Foraging

Characters must eat and drink to function. Without adequate rations:

- **No food**: Lose 1 HP per day. No natural healing while starving.
- **No water**: Lose 1d4 HP per day. Dangerous in arid environments.

::: {.callout-important title="Foraging"}
Once per day, the party makes a single [Search/Listen](skills.md#search-listen) roll at the highest Search/Listen chance in the party. On success, the foragers find food and water enough for **1d6 characters** for one day.
:::

## Wilderness Encounter Checks

::: {.callout-important title="Wilderness Encounters"}
Check for encounters **twice per day**: once during the day's travel and once during the night watch.

Roll 1d6: encounter on a **1**. In dangerous territories, the referee may roll on a **1–2**.
:::

Wilderness encounters vary by terrain and region — beasts, patrols, merchants, refugees, or rivals. The referee prepares encounter tables appropriate to the area.

When a check indicates an encounter, follow the [Encounter Sequence](encounters.md#encounter-sequence).

## Rest and Night Camps

Characters must sleep approximately 8 hours per day or accumulate fatigue (**−1** to all rolls per missed full rest).

::: {.callout-important title="Camp & Watch"}
Making camp takes **2 turns**. Establish a watch rotation: **3 watches per 8-hour rest**, with an encounter check each watch. If an encounter occurs, sleeping characters are surprised unless the watchperson succeeds on a [Search/Listen](skills.md#search-listen) roll.
:::

## Overland Navigation Aids

| Aid | Effect |
|-----|--------|
| Clear sky (sun/stars) | Can navigate accurately; no lost check penalty |
| Map and compass | +1 to avoid getting lost in known territory |
| Local guide | Ignore lost checks entirely; may know shortcuts |
| Landmark visible | Negate one lost check |
