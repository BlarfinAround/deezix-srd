# Hit Points and Death

A character's **hit points (HP)** measure their capacity to withstand harm.

## Hit Dice

Characters gain HP by rolling their class's **Hit Die** at each level and adding their CON modifier to each roll. The minimum result per die is **1** (a character always gains at least 1 HP per level).

| Class | Hit Die |
|-------|---------|
| Fighter | d8 |
| Cleric | d6 |
| Magic-User | d4 |
| Thief | d6 |

::: {.callout-note title="Example: Rolling Hit Points"}
A 1st-level Fighter with CON 15 (+1) rolls a d8 and gets 4. Adding +1 gives 5 HP. At 2nd level they roll again: 7 + 1 = 8 additional HP, for a total of 13.
:::

## Death

::: {.callout-important title="Death"}
A character at **0 HP** is dead, unless the attack was declared as a [subdual](combat.md#subduing).
:::

## Healing

### Natural Healing

::: {.callout-important title="Natural Healing"}
A character who takes a **full rest** (at least 8 hours of sleep with no strenuous activity) recovers **1 HP**. A full day of bed rest recovers **1d3 HP**.
:::

### Magical Healing
Spells such as *Cure Light Wounds* restore HP immediately. Magical healing cannot raise a character above their maximum HP. See [Divine Spells](../magic/divine-spells.md).

## Monsters and Death

Monsters die at **0 HP** unless [subdued](combat.md#subduing).

## Environmental Damage

Hazards outside of weapon attacks deal damage directly to HP:

| Type | Damage |
|------|--------|
| Fire (sustained) | 1d6/round; burning oil: 1d8 direct, then 1d4 for 2 rounds |
| Falling | 1d6 per 10' (max 20d6) |
| Drowning | 1d6/round after breath runs out |
