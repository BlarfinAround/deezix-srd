# Monster Statistics

Every monster is described with a standardized stat block. Field reference below.

## AC — Armor Class
See [Armor Class](../../character/equipment/weapons-and-armor.md#armor-class).

## HD — Hit Dice
Number of d8 hit dice, with the average HP in parentheses. Roll or use the average.

- **HD X+Y / X−Y**: add or subtract Y from the rolled total (minimum 1 HP).
- **HD ½**: counts as 1 HD for any rule that asks for HD.
- **HD X\***: each asterisk marks one notable special ability (see [Experience and Advancement](../../character/experience-and-advancement.md)).

## AB — Attack Bonus
Added to the monster's d20 attack roll.

| Monster HD | AB |
|------------|----|
| ½–1        | +0 |
| 2          | +1 |
| 3          | +2 |
| 4          | +3 |
| 5          | +4 |
| 6+         | +5 |

## Att — Attacks
Listed as **count × type (damage)**, e.g., **2 × claw (1d6)**. Special attacks appear as separate trait lines.

## MV — Movement
Feet per dungeon turn, with feet per combat round in parentheses: **MV 120' (40')**. Additional modes follow in sequence, e.g., **(Fly 180' (60'))**.

## SV — Saving Throws
Five values in canonical order: **D**eath · **W**ands · **P**aralysis · **B**reath · **S**pells. Monsters save as a **Fighter equal to their HD**. Exceptions are noted in the trait list.

## ML — Morale
2d6 score from 4 (cowardly) to 11 (fearless). See [Reactions, Morale, and Loyalty](../reactions-morale-loyalty.md). Mindless monsters list `—` and never check.

## AL — Alignment
Lawful (L), Neutral (N), or Chaotic (C). Two values mean behavior varies.

## XP — Experience
Awarded for defeating the monster. See [Experience and Advancement](../../character/experience-and-advancement.md) for the table.

## NA — Number Appearing
Two rolls in the form **XdY (AdB)**: dungeon encounter, then wilderness lair. A leading 1 with no roll means a fixed single encounter.

## TT — Treasure Type
A letter code keyed to the [Treasure Types](../treasure.md#treasure-types) tables. **X (Y)** means individuals carry X, the lair holds Y. A dash means no treasure.
