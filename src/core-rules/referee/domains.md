# Domains

From around 9th level, a character may carve out a **domain** — a holding that draws followers and earns income between adventures.

## Strongholds

A character who clears a region of monsters and builds a stronghold attracts followers and revenue. Clearing means eliminating or driving out hostile creatures within the surrounding area (typically a 6-mile radius).

### Construction Costs

Typical structure costs; a keep requires **one engineer per 100,000 gp** of construction cost.

| Class      | Stronghold          | Cost      | Build Time        |
| ---------- | ------------------- | --------- | ----------------- |
| Fighter    | Keep                | 75,000 gp | ~5 months         |
| Cleric     | Temple or shrine    | Referee sets (halved if in divine favour) | 1 day per 500 gp |
| Magic-User | Large tower         | 30,000 gp | ~2 months         |
| Thief      | Guild hall/hideout  | Referee sets to setting | — |

Build time is **1 in-game day per 500 gp** of total cost.

## Followers

On completing construction, followers arrive to serve the character.

| Class      | Followers                                                          |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Fighter    | Referee determines types and numbers; typically soldiers and cavalry |
| Cleric     | 5d6 × 10 fighters of level 1–2, completely devoted (never check morale) |
| Magic-User | 1d6 apprentices of levels 1–3                                      |
| Thief      | 2d6 1st-level thieves; may form the nucleus of a guild             |

Followers stay loyal so long as they are led, paid, and housed.

## Income

Characters who settle the domain receive **10 gp per year per settler household**. The referee determines how many settlers arrive and at what rate based on the region's safety, wealth, and the character's reputation.

This rate is deliberately slow — domain income is a long-term reward for sustained high-level play, not a windfall. Supplement it with trade routes, tolls, or tithes as the setting allows.

## Downtime

High-level play unfolds largely between adventures — building, ruling, researching, training. Run downtime at a 1:1 scale of real days to game days where possible, so a character spending weeks on construction or magical research stays in step with the party.
