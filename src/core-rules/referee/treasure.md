## Treasure Types

Monster stat blocks reference treasure by **letter code**. The format `TT X (Y)` in a stat block means **individuals carry X**, and **the lair holds Y**. Roll the indicated probability for each row; on a success, roll the listed quantity.

Only the treasure types used by monsters in this SRD are listed. The codes follow classic conventions for OSR compatibility.

## Gems and Jewelry

Gems and jewelry provide dense, portable wealth. The referee sets value based on quality and the setting.

**Gem Value Table (roll d20)**:

| d20 | Value |
|-----|-------|
| 1–4 | 10 gp |
| 5–9 | 50 gp |
| 10–15 | 100 gp |
| 16–19 | 500 gp |
| 20 | 1,000 gp |

**Jewelry**: 3d6 × 100 gp.

## Cursed Items

Cursed items are indistinguishable from normal magic items until used. They impose penalties and usually require *Remove Curse* to discard.

**Examples:**

- **−1 weapons/armor**: Reduces rather than improves performance.
- **Bag of Devouring**: Functions as a bag of holding until the owner puts something valuable inside.
- **Berserker sword**: Forces the wielder to attack the nearest creature each round.

## Identifying Magic Items

The properties of magic items are not automatically identified. Common methods:

- *Detect Magic* confirms an item is magical but not its function.
- **Arcane Lore** (Magic-User skill) attempts to identify an item's properties with a chance of success.
- A **sage** in town can identify an item for 100–500 gp.
- **Trial and error**: use it and observe.

Magic items should not be available for sale in shops.
