# Morale and Loyalty

These procedures cover when monsters break and flee, and how retainers stay loyal to the party. For the encounter-time **reaction roll**, see [Reactions](../adventuring/encounters.md#reactions) in the Encounters chapter.

## Morale

Morale determines when monsters and NPCs **break and flee** rather than fight to the death. Player characters are never subject to morale.

Each monster has a **Morale** score from 4 (cowardly) to 11 (fearless), with 7–8 as average. Mindless undead, golems, and constructs **never check morale**.

### When to Check

Check morale once at each of these moments:

1. **First casualty** in the group.
2. **Half strength** lost.
3. **Leader falls** or is incapacitated.
4. **Unexpected threat** — a dramatically more powerful force appears.

### Making a Morale Check

::: {.callout-important title="Morale Check"}
Roll **2d6**. If the result is **equal to or less than** the Morale score, the group holds. If it **exceeds** the score, they break.
:::

A group that breaks will **flee**, **surrender**, or **cower**, depending on the creature's nature and situation.

## Loyalty

Retainers have a **Loyalty** score that functions as their personal morale — checked when they face serious danger or are given distasteful orders. Base Loyalty is determined by their employer's CHA (see [Retainers and Hirelings](../character/equipment/retainers-and-hirelings.md)) and adjusts over play.

### Loyalty Adjustments

Cumulative, at the referee's discretion:

- Treated generously (bonus pay, shared equipment): **+1**
- The party saved the retainer's life: **+1**
- Treated poorly (withheld pay, used as bait): **−1**
- Killed through party carelessness: **−2**

A retainer whose Loyalty drops to 3 or lower leaves at the first safe opportunity — possibly after stealing something valuable.

### Checking Loyalty

Roll the retainer's Loyalty as a [morale check](#making-a-morale-check) when they face serious danger. Retainers who flee may return after the danger passes.
