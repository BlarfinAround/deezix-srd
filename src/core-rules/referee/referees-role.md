# Referee's Role

The referee's job is to describe the world, adjudicate the rules fairly, and let the players explore.

## Core Principles

- **Rulings over rules**: Make fair, fast rulings when the rules don't cover something. Use an X-in-6 chance if uncertainty is involved. Write it down if it'll recur.
- **The world is real**: Monsters and NPCs have routines and motivations.
- **Let players fail**: Failure should be possible and consequential. Don't fudge dice.
- **Reward clever play**: Preparation, information-gathering, and lateral thinking should work.

## Chance Rolls and Ability Checks

X-in-6 chance rolls are a quick way to handle uncertain situations not covered by the rules.

| Target | % Chance | Likelihood |
| ------ | :------- | ---------- |
| 1-in-6 | 16.7%    | Highly unlikely |
| 2-in-6 | 33.3%    | Probably not |
| 3-in-6 | 50.0%    | 50/50 |
| 4-in-6 | 66.7%    | Probably |
| 5-in-6 | 83.3%    | Highly likely |

**Ability Checks**: Ability scores may be used to determine a character's chance of succeeding tasks not covered by Skills. In these cases, start with a base **2-in-6 chance** and apply the relevant ability modifier.

## Monster Behavior

Monsters have lairs, routines, and motivations. They:

- Respond to noise (investigate after a nearby fight).
- May retreat to get reinforcements.
- Will negotiate if it serves their interests.
- Pursue fleeing parties to the edge of their territory.

If the party kills the goblins in room 4, the ogre they were paying tribute to in room 9 will notice.

## Running Encounters

1. Establish the scene.
2. Roll 2d6 for monster reaction if outcome isn't predetermined (see [Reactions, Morale, and Loyalty](reactions-morale-loyalty.md)).
3. Give players a moment to act before the first blow if monsters are Neutral or better.
4. Check morale at appropriate moments. Let creatures flee.

## Time Keeping

| Unit | Use |
|------|-----|
| Round (~10 sec) | Combat |
| Turn (10 min) | Dungeon exploration |
| Hour | Rest, short travel |
| Day | Wilderness travel, healing, downtime |
| Week | Magical research, construction |

Activities like magical research and recuperating from serious hit point loss or harmful effects  can take several weeks. It is encouraged to allow these activities to take place between game sessions using a 1:1 time scale of real days to game days.
