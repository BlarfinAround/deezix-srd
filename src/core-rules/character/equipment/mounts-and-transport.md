# Mounts and Transport

Mounts, vehicles, and boats let the party move faster, carry more, and travel by water.

## Animals

| Animal | Cost | Capacity | Exploration Speed | Wilderness Speed | Notes |
|--------|------|----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Riding horse | 75 gp | 15 | — | 60 miles/day | Fast overland; cannot enter dungeons |
| Warhorse | 250 gp | 20 | — | 40 miles/day | Combat-trained; carries rider in full plate |
| Draft horse | 40 gp | 40 | — | 30 miles/day | Work animal; needed for carts and wagons |
| Mule | 30 gp | 30 | 60'/turn | 20 miles/day | Sturdy; can navigate narrow paths; slower than horses |

**Wilderness Speed** reflects sustained daily travel. See [Wilderness Travel](../../adventuring/wilderness-travel.md) for rules on getting lost, terrain penalties, and survival.

## Animal Upkeep

Mounts and animals cost money to maintain:

| Animal | Feed/Stabling (per day) |
|--------|------------------------|
| Horse (any) | 5 sp |
| Mule | 3 sp |

Animals must be fed. In the wilderness, the referee may allow a foraging attempt to find grazing and water for up to 2 horses.

## Mounted Combat

A character on horseback may:

- Attack with melee weapons from the saddle (+2 attack against unmounted opponents).
- Attack with ranged weapons (−2 attack if moving).
- Use a lance at 1d12 damage.

Horses will not willingly enter dungeons, tight passages, or areas with strong monster scent. A warhorse can be trained to fight in open combat but still refuses enclosed spaces.

## Travel by Water

Boats and ships follow their speed in miles per day under normal conditions. The referee may apply movement penalties or bonuses based on wind and weather conditions.
