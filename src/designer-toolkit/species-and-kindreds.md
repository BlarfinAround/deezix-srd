# Species and Kindreds

By default, Deezix has no character species system. This section gives three models for adding non-human characters when a setting calls for them. Choose one and apply it consistently.

## The Three Models

### Model 1: Narrative-Only

Species has no mechanical effect. An elf and a human are mechanically identical; the difference lives in fiction, names, and roleplay.

### Model 2: Class-Based (Recommended)

Each species is a unique class built with the [class design framework](designing-classes.md). Playing a dwarf means playing the Dwarf class — the species *is* the class, not a layer on top of it.

This model fits the chassis most cleanly.

### Model 3: Trait-Based

Species is a separate choice from class, adding a small package of modifiers on top of the character's class.

A trait-based species entry contains:

1. **Ability score modifications** — usually ±1 to one or two scores. Cap each score at 3–18 after modification.
2. **One or two minor traits** — drawn from the trait guidelines below.
3. **Other drawbacks** — weaknesses to balance if the package is otherwise net positive.

Trait packages should be net-zero where possible. Positive-only packages require a matching "default" species package and careful consideration of how the bonus interacts with each class.

**Trait guidelines.** Species traits should use mechanics that already exist in the chassis.

- **Ability score modifiers**: net zero or slightly positive across all changed scores.
- **X-in-6 skill bonuses**: +1 to a named skill in a *named circumstance* ("+1 to Search/Listen for hidden doors", "+1 to Stealth in natural terrain"). Avoid unconditional skill bumps.
- **Save modifiers**: +1 to one of the five save categories, or +1 against a named effect type within a category ("+1 to Spells saves vs. charm effects"). Keep modifiers within ±1.
- **Damage interactions**: half damage from a given type, or — rarely — immunity to a narrow non-combat hazard. Reserve immunity for narrow sources.
- **Movement**: ±10' to base movement rate.

::: {.callout-note title="Example: Elf"}

- **Ability Scores**: +1 INT, −1 STR, −1 CON
- **Infravision** (60').
- **Keen Senses**: +1 to Search/Listen rolls to spot hidden doors or concealed creatures.
- **Fey-Touched**: +1 to Spells saves against charm and compulsion effects.
- **Skills**: −1 to Break.
:::
